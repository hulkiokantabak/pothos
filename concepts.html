Experimental Concepts

One open word, taken all the way to a finished, honest, self-contained thing.

Each piece in this series starts from a single word and is built end to end — interpreted, argued over by panels designed to disagree, fact-checked, audited, and published — without copying the one before it. The method stays; the shape changes every time. A gallery, then a game, then a language — and now a garden that runs on the calendar.

I · KALÓN — beauty

Beauty is not one thing; it is a few recurring moves, and the last move belongs to the beholder. Five playable generative instruments and a finale composed from your own play.

hulkiokantabak.github.io/kalon

II · ROLLO — courage

Courage after Rollo May: not the absence of fear but movement in spite of doubt and despair — played, not preached. A wordless marsh crossing where the only certain road goes backward. Its first version was a text page; the author ruled it a failure, and the lesson — text depicts, mechanics enact — shaped everything since.

hulkiokantabak.github.io/rollo

III · LEXIS — curiosity

Curiosity is the loop by which a mind teaches itself a world it was not given the key to. A small language you read by writing it: you arrange glyphs, the field answers with the state they mean, and you revise. Nothing is hidden; the grammar's depth simply does not run out.

hulkiokantabak.github.io/lexis

IV · POTHOS — desire (you are here)

Desire lived in one register: the distance between wanting and having. A garden of appointments — you sow a seed, it names its date, and nothing you do can hurry it. The first piece in the series that refuses to finish in one sitting: it happens mostly while the tab is closed, it can be genuinely missed, and it can be addressed to another person and sent as a letter. The prior three concepts each completed on screen; this one blooms whether or not anyone is watching.
